How Labs works

Market testing done honestly.

Most "user research" is confirmation bias in a trench coat. We do it differently. Here's the actual process.

01
You join and we listen
When you sign up, you tell us about yourself — what you use, what frustrates you, what you've tried and abandoned. We read every single response. This isn't a database segmentation exercise. We're looking for problems.
02
We bring you real questions
Not surveys. Not focus groups. We'll reach out directly when we're working through a specific question — "how do you currently handle X?" or "what would make you switch away from the thing you use for Y?" Real conversations, not checkbox forms.
03
We show you rough work
Labs members see concepts before they're polished. Early screen designs, unfinished flows, competing approaches we're unsure about. Your reaction to rough work is more honest than your reaction to a finished product.
04
We tell you what we did with it
After every research cycle, we share what we heard, what changed as a result, and what we decided not to do despite hearing it — and why. Accountability goes both ways. If you give us your time, we owe you transparency about what happened to it.

The honest bit.

Labs isn't an advisory board. We won't always do what you ask. Sometimes what users say they want and what actually helps them are different things — and it's our job to figure out which is which.

What we commit to: we'll always tell you what we heard, what we built based on it, and what we chose to ignore and why. You'll never wonder what happened to the thing you suggested.

We also won't pretend that the research is the decider. The builder is the decider. Research reduces risk. It doesn't eliminate judgement.
